Conclusion

The distribution of paper quantity is generally positively correlated with the number of individuals. Among these, the United States, Australia, the United Kingdom, and France stand out as the main contributing countries.

Whether the cohort is from Greece or other countries, there is a substantial representation in the field of Engineering & Technology and Medical & Health Sciences. However, Greece has a relatively higher number of individuals in the field of Medical & Health Sciences.

Clinical Medicine, Biomedical Research, and Information & Communication Technologies are the most populous groups in terms of numbers.

Analyzing the distribution of the scientific workforce using charts can offer significant insights for shaping national policies and guiding research directions. This becomes particularly relevant for a country aiming to recover from prolonged economic challenges compounded by the recent COVID-19 pandemic. Recognizing and harnessing its scientific potential, reversing the trend of brain drain, and making well-informed decisions at the intersection of science and society can contribute substantial additional value.
